Frequently Asked Questions

You can join Dragonborn through our Discord Server. Follow the recruitment process outlined there to become a member.

The ranking system consists of three groups:
  • Lower Echelon (Initiate to Vanguard)
  • Central Assembly (Legionary to Dragonborn)
  • Upper Echelon (Emperor/Empress)
You can progress through Lower Echelon and Central Assembly by attending warfare events and assessments. Upper Echelon positions are obtained through applications or being chosen by the Council.

You earn 1 XP for each warfare event or assessment you attend. Required XP for ranks:
  • Neophyte: Purchase uniform from group store
  • Ascendant: 2 events
  • Sentinel: 4 events
  • Crusader: 8 events
  • Vanguard: 16 events
  • Legionary: 38 events
  • Arcane: 76 events
  • Blademaster: 124 events
  • Dragonborn: 200 events

Members must remain active within a two-week period. If you need to be inactive, you must submit an inactivity notice through the #directory channel in Discord. Those inactive for over two weeks without notice will be exiled from the group.

Dragonborn has two elite divisions:
  • Wyverns: Elite members who demonstrate exceptional skills, activity, loyalty, and maturity
  • Dragon Guard: The highest prestigious division, chosen for their outstanding activity, skill, loyalty, and devotion to the group

Dragonborn has several departments:
  • Community Department: Organizes events and fosters community engagement
  • Justice Department: Protects Dragonborn from internal/external threats
  • Warfare Department: Handles internal/external operations and warfare events
  • Pyro Foundry: Official development team for Dragonborn experiences
  • Covert Operations: Elite task force for raiding and defending

Dragonborn maintains three types of disciplinary records:
  • Blacklists: Applied to groups that violate our policies
  • Suspensions: Temporary restrictions with specific durations
  • Global Bans: Permanent restrictions from all Dragonborn activities
All disciplinary actions are documented with evidence and can be viewed in our public registries.
